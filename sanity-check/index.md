Got a quote, proposal, or contract from a web agency sitting in your inbox?
Before you sign anything, it's worth getting an independent second opinion
from someone who isn't trying to sell you a website.

That's what a **Web Project Sanity Check** is: I review what you've been
sent, then we get on a call to go through it together — before you commit
your budget.

It's particularly useful if:

- You've had a quote and aren't sure if it's reasonable.
- A contract has clauses you don't fully understand.
- You just want someone experienced to check your thinking before you sign.

### How it works

1. **You send me the material** — whatever you've got: your brief, the
   agency's proposal, the quote, the contract.
2. **I read it properly** — I spend an hour going through everything in
   detail, checking for hidden costs, ownership traps, vague deliverables,
   and anything that doesn't add up.
3. **We talk it through** — a one-hour video call where I walk you through
   what I found, flag any red flags, and help you work out the right
   follow-up questions to ask the agency.
